Save Central Foothills

Boise, ID

The protection of open space and thoughtful, measured development in our precious Boise foothills is an issue that concerns many Boise residents. People come from far and wide to access these foothills.

While responsible development is welcomed, many of your neighbors have come together to save an at-risk area in Quail Hollow from poorly designed development. Our campaign, "Save Central Foothills", needs your help to save important open spaces in the area.

UPDATE 08/20: Idaho Statesman story: "New Boise Foothills subdivision could add 30 homes. Neighbors see threat to open space"

  • The Development

The Development

A subdivision of two separate parcels is being proposed as shown on this map between Quail Hollow Golf Course and Hillside to Hollow Reserve. The parcels are very steep and also are enjoyed by many trail users every day.

The proposal would include a new road through an open space lot and the grading required for 30 lots.

  • What are the issues?

What are the issues?

  1. An Open Space Lot Would Be Developed

    The proposed subdivision would include developing an Open Space lot within Eyrie Canyon Subdivision, designated as ‘Unbuildable’ during Eyrie Canyon’s approval in 2009. (Blue parcel on map)  The intent of the Planning & Zoning Commission and citizens at that time was this lot was to remain Open Space forever.   Allowing development of an Open Space lot would set a very bad precedent for the entire City.

  2. Nearby Homeowners Told Lot Was Too Steep

    When selling homes, neighbors were told by the developer that the Open Space lot was just too steep to do anything with.  They purchased their lots and invested in building a home using that representation.

  3. Leveling Our Foothills

    The project does not comply with the City's development rules.  Most of both parcels are steeper than 25% and beyond the threshold for development in the City's Blueprint Boise Plan and Hillside Development Ordinance. Where cut and fill are to be minimized in the City's plans, this project would cut 50' off the top off a prominent ridge and fill gullies, some with as much as 80' of dirt. That is the equivalent of a 7 story building!  This is not our community's vision for responsible foothills development.

  4. Fire Danger and Access

    Fire danger in the foothills is a real threat to homes, people and firefighters.    There is only one street in/out with no secondary access.
